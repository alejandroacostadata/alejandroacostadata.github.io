INEGI Labor Market Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of the Mexican labor market using ENOE survey data with interactive dashboards for employment patterns and economic insights

Project Overview

This project analyzes the main characteristics of the Mexican labor market to identify patterns indicating potential distortions, the relationship between years of schooling and employment activities, as well as income in relation to years of preparation and work experience. Developed for INEGI (National Institute of Statistics and Geography), this interactive platform processes data from the ENOE (National Survey of Occupation and Employment) covering multiple quarters from 2019 to 2022, including pre-pandemic and pandemic scenarios.

Statistical Capabilities Highlights

The platform implements advanced statistical analysis techniques for labor market research:

  • Data Processing: Custom education level mapping with years of schooling calculations
  • Work Experience Calculation: Derived variable creation based on age, education, and employment history
  • Multi-dimensional Analysis: Cross-tabulation of education, occupation, income, and demographics
  • Time Series Analysis: Quarterly comparisons with pandemic impact assessment
  • Interactive Visualization: Dynamic charts with drill-down capabilities and real-time filtering
  • Statistical Measures: Mean, median, and distribution analysis across multiple variables

Key Variables & Analysis

The analysis incorporates comprehensive labor market variables from ENOE survey data, providing insights into employment patterns, educational mismatches, and income distribution.

üìä Primary Variables

Demographics: Age, sex, and geographic location of employed individuals.

Education: Custom nomenclature mapping school years (none=0 to doctorate=23 years).

Occupation: SINCO classification system with skill level integration (Levels 1-4).

Employment: Position in occupation, time in last job, and employment formality status.

üîÑ Derived Variables

Work Experience: Calculated from age minus education years plus 6 (school entry age).

Education-Occupation Match: Analysis of educational level appropriateness for occupation type.

Income Analysis: Monthly income distribution by education, experience, and position.

Job Stability: Time working in last job categorized into discrete periods.
